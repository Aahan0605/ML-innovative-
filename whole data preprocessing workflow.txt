====================================================================
      WHOLE DATA PREPROCESSING WORKFLOW - REDDIT COLLAPSE PROJECT
====================================================================

GOAL: Convert granular, post-level interaction data (millions of rows) into a structured, time-series historical timeline suitable for predicting the collapse of online communities.

WHY WE NEED TO DO THIS:
Machine learning models (like Random Forests, LSTMs, and XGBoost) cannot look at a single post (e.g., "Dragon Ball' Creator Akira Toryiyama Has Passed Away") and classify whether the entire anime subreddit is collapsing. They need historical "snapshots" or trends of the community's health over time. We must distill millions of noisy posts into clean, monthly community metrics.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
STEP-BY-STEP WORKFLOW & JUSTIFICATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------

1. DATA INGESTION & BATCH PROCESSING
======================================
Procedure: Read all 50 separate CSV files iteratively instead of loading them all into memory at once.
Why we do this: Loading 50 large datasets simultaneously would crash the system (out of RAM). Processing file-by-file ensures our pipeline is memory-efficient and scalable to larger datasets (e.g., millions of rows from big subreddits).

2. FILTERING OUT NOISE (Data Cleaning)
======================================
Procedure: Exclude rows where `is_bot == True`.
Why we do this: We are trying to predict HUMAN community collapse. Bots automatically posting or replying artificially inflate engagement metrics. If we don't remove them, a community might look highly active when it is actually dead and only populated by auto-moderators.

3. TEMPORAL ABSTRACTION (Time-stamping)
=======================================
Procedure: Convert the `created_utc` column from a raw UTC string to a standard Datetime object, then extract the Year and Month (e.g., "2024-03").
Why we do this: Granular timestamps (down to the second) are too chaotic. To track "decline" or "collapse", we need to look at macro-level trends. Monthly grouping is the industry standard for longitudinal behavioral analysis.

4. AGGREGATION & FEATURE EXTRACTION
===================================
Procedure: Group the cleaned data by `subreddit` and `year_month`, and extract statistical metrics describing that specific month's activity:
   * Total Posts (Count of IDs) -> Measures Volume
   * Total Comments (Sum of `num_comments`) -> Measures Direct Engagement
   * Average Score & Upvote Ratio -> Measures Content Quality & Consensus
Why we do this: These engineered macro variables are the actual "signals" of community health. For example, a sharp drop in Total Comments while Total Posts stays high indicates that people are still posting, but no one cares enough to reply—an early signal of collapse.

5. CONCATENATION & EXPORT
=========================
Procedure: Combine all the monthly summaries from all 50 subreddits into a single tabular dataset (`processed_subreddit_time_series.csv`).
Why we do this: This final CSV is our "Master Dataset". Instead of parsing through raw text and timestamps for every ML experiment, we now have a lightweight, mathematically dense matrix where every row represents one month of a community's life. 

--------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY THIS PREPROCESSED DATA IS THE *BEST* DATA FOR YOUR PROBLEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------
By organizing the data sequentially by month per subreddit, we have mathematically modeled "Time". This setup allows us to look at Month T-3, Month T-2, and Month T-1 to predict Month T (which is exactly how you promised the faculty you would predict failure *before* it happens!). 

It shifts the focus from "what is the community talking about?" (Social Media Analysis) to "how is the community behaving over time?" (Structural & Behavioral Collapse Prediction).
